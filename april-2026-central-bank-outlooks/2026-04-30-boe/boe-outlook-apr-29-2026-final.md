## Macro Backdrop

As of Apr 30, 2026 (T-1 FINAL), the BoE MPC enters the April 30 decision in a structurally bifurcated state: 4 hawk / 1 neutral / 4 dove on stance, but with both the hawk and dove tails materially firmer than at campaign start (Apr 12) due to the Iran energy shock cycle. Full purdah has held since Apr 23-24 — no MPC member on-record rate commentary in 7 days. The February 5-4 split (doves wanted to cut) collapsed to a unanimous 9-0 hold in March; the Apr 30 modal vote pattern is now NOT 9-0 but 7-2 hold with Pill+Mann hike-dissent.

Hawks (Pill, Mann, Greene, Lombardelli) have been validated by the Apr 22 March CPI print (3.3% headline, 4.5% services HAWKISH BEAT on BoE's stickiness gauge), the Apr 23 PMI 28-year-high input-price reading, the Apr 25 DMP 4.0% one-year-ahead inflation expectations (up from 3.5% March), and the Apr 29 FOMC 4-dissent fragmentation (highest since 1992 — three hawks dissented on easing-bias language while voting with hold rate). Brent ~$120/bbl Apr 29 (highest since June 2022), UAE-OPEC exit effective May 1, and the Hormuz blockade-deadlock all reinforce the energy-shock-as-persistent (not transient) framing.

Doves (Ramsden, Breeden, Dhingra, Taylor) have been counterbalanced by the GfK -25 / CBI -68 demand-destruction sequence — UK retail-sales-volume confidence at a 40-year series low, consumer confidence at the largest monthly drop in a year. Their argument: the energy shock is being absorbed by demand destruction rather than re-embedding inflation persistence. BRC April shop-price inflation easing to 1.0% y/y (discount-driven) is marginal dove-assist on the same channel.

Bailey's 'won't rush either way' / 'studious neutrality' / 'very, very difficult' framing has held throughout the campaign and now has explicit peer-CB cover from Powell's Apr 29 'look through oil shock but very cautious' framing. Pill's Apr 17 IMF pushback ('if you're waiting and seeing and you don't see, then you've just waited') has only sharpened given the FOMC 4-dissent precedent.

The most likely Apr 30 outcome is hold at 3.75% with a split vote: 7-2 hold w/ Pill+Mann hike-dissent (~0.45-0.55) is modal; 8-1 hold w/ Pill alone hike-dissent (~0.25-0.30); unanimous 9-0 hold (~0.10-0.15); 6-2-1 (Pill+Mann hike + Dhingra cut) tail (~0.05-0.10). Statement tone likely emphasizes BOTH 'second-round risks' AND 'demand-side restraint' — balanced read giving MPR fan-charts maximum optionality. The MPR (released day-of) is the dominant signal: fan-chart CPI path through 2026 decides whether next move is up, hold-extended, or down.

## Institutional Dynamic

*Context that the equilibrium math does not capture but should inform interpretation:*

The BoE April 30 meeting includes the Monetary Policy Report (MPR), which means new forecasts and fan charts. The MPR will incorporate the first post-Iran macro projections, which could shift the narrative significantly depending on the inflation and growth outlook.

If the MPR projects CPI above 3% through 2026, hawks gain leverage; if it projects a growth slowdown absorbing the inflation, doves gain leverage. After the FOMC 4-dissent precedent on Apr 29, the BoE has explicit external evidence that energy-shock 'look-through' framing is not consensus-stable across G3 committees — Pill+Mann hike-dissent is structurally easier to defend in MPR-companion communications.

The MPR is often the single biggest input to MPC decisions at these meetings — more than individual member speeches.
