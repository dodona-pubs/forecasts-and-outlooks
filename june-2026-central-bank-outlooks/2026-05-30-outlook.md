### How to read these

Dodona reasons from **fundamentals** — the institutional-actor
equilibrium (each voter's stance, confidence-weighted), the policy cycle,
the macro regime, and indicator signals. It does **not** read prediction
markets into the forecast; markets (Polymarket/Kalshi) are used only as an
independent comparison and are not yet ingested for June (a T-7 step). All
actor stances are derived from pre-decision communications under strict
temporal holdout (no evidence dated after the cutoff enters the math).

## Coverage & data-quality notes (honest)

These are real limitations in today's substrate; surfaced rather than hidden.

- **FOMC roster — Powell-era, Warsh transition not modeled.** The single
  largest gap. FOMC's number is non-committal pending the Warsh roster +
  speech substrate. (See FOMC caveat.)
- **Stance-substrate breadth.** The directional equilibrium is driven by the
  voters with recent stance signals: ECB 6 of 21, BoJ 4 of 10, FOMC 7 of ~12,
  BoE 6 of 9. Voters without recent communications carry forward neutral and
  contribute little directional weight — a coverage gap, not a claim those
  members are neutral.
- **ECB unemployment is stale** (6.7%, 2023-02 print) — the `ea-unemployment`
  series needs refresh. It does not drive the ECB hold (rate/inflation/stance
  do), but the displayed value is old.
- **Prediction-market comparison absent.** No June Polymarket/Kalshi markets
  are ingested yet; the model-vs-market cross-check is a T-7 step. Markets are
  comparison-only and never enter the forecast math.
- **Numeric precision.** Some indicator values are shown rounded; the engine
  carries full precision internally.
